Mjolnir's Meat Feast

Greetings, honored feasters! As I stood before the roaring flames, tending to the meats fit for the Allfather Odin himself, a sense of anticipation filled the air. The scents of roasting wild boar, smoked venison, and lamb carried the essence of adventure and valor. As the meal came together, I couldn't help but reflect on the great feasts of the gods and the stories woven into the tapestry of Norse mythology.

Upon presenting this platter to Odin, his eye twinkled with appreciation. His heart, like the fires of our hearth, warmed with the taste of the feast before him. The honey-glazed carrots and parsnips, a tribute to his kinship with the earth, brought a sense of balance to the hearty meats. The Feast of Mjölnir was a success, a harmonious melody of flavors that echoed the resilience and strength of the Norse gods.

May this feast inspire you to gather your kin and embark on your own culinary adventure, honoring the legacy of the gods and savoring the essence of the Northlands.

Ingrediants:

  • 1 wild boar roast (5-6 lbs)
  • 1 venison roast (4-5 lbs)
  • 1 lamb leg (3-4 lbs)
  • 4 large carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 4 parsnips, peeled and sliced
  • 1/2 cup honey
  • 4 tbsp butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh rosemary sprigs
  • Fresh thyme sprigs

  1. Prepare the Meats:

    2.

    Marinate the wild boar, venison, and lamb with a mix of olive oil, salt, pepper, fresh thyme, and rosemary. Allow them to marinate for at least 4 hours or overnight for the best flavor.

  2. Preheat The Grill:

    3.

    Prepare your grill for indirect grilling over medium heat. If using a charcoal grill, place the coals on one side of the grill to create a hot zone and a cooler zone. If using a gas grill, turn on only one burner to achieve the same effect.

  3. Grill the Meats:

    4.

    Place the marinated meats on the cooler side of the grill, away from direct heat. Close the lid and cook for about 1.5 to 2 hours, rotating the meats occasionally for even cooking. Keep an eye on the internal temperature with a meat thermometer. The wild boar and venison should reach an internal temperature of 145°F (63°C), while the lamb should reach 135°F (57°C) for medium-rare. Adjust the cooking time accordingly.

  4. Honey-Glazed Carrots and Parsnips:

    5.

    In a saucepan, melt the butter and add honey. Stir until well combined. Add the sliced carrots and parsnips to the saucepan, coating them with the honey-butter mixture. Cook over medium heat for about 15-20 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender and caramelized. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  5. Assemble the Feast:

    6.

    Arrange the roasted wild boar, smoked venison, and roasted lamb on a Viking-sized platter. Carve the meats into hearty slices.

  6. Serve:

    7.

    Serve the meats alongside the honey-glazed carrots and parsnips. Garnish the platter with fresh thyme and rosemary sprigs for an extra touch of Norse authenticity.
